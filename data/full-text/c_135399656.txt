2016-05-30

 Singapore leaders congratulate incoming Philippine president



SINGAPORE, May 30 (Xinhua) -- Singapore President Tony Tan Keng Yam and Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong have written to president-elect of the Republic of the Philippines Rodrigo Duterte, to congratulate him on winning the Philippines Presidential Election, following the official announcement by the Congress of the Philippines, said the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA) in a statement on Monday.
Saying that Singapore and the Philippines are "old friends with strong ties," President Tan extended an invitation to Duterte to visit Singapore.
"Our countries cooperate closely in ASEAN and internationally, including at the United Nations, to promote peace, stability and prosperity," Tan also wrote.
Singapore Prime Minister Lee said that Singapore and the Philippines share close ties spanning many sectors, including trade and investment, culture and people-to-people exchanges.
"Our bilateral relationship will reach a milestone in 2019 when we celebrate 50 years of diplomatic ties. As fellow founding members of ASEAN, we cooperate closely to promote regional peace and prosperity. Singapore looks forward to the Philippines' chairmanship of ASEAN in 2017, which is also the 50th anniversary of the founding of ASEAN. I am confident that we will be able to foster closer ASEAN integration amid a complex and fast-changing global landscape," Lee wrote.
